### 2. Environmental Triggers and Misattribution

Some field studies have noted that areas associated with haunting experiences often exhibit **unusual magnetic fields** (either higher than average strength or, more commonly, high **variability/fluctuation**) compared to non-haunted areas. These unusual fields may be caused by:

* **Geological factors** (e.g., fault lines, magnetic minerals).
* **Faulty or old electrical wiring** in a building.
* **Natural fluctuations** in the Earth's geomagnetic field.

In this model, the **EMF is not the ghost**; the EMF is a **stressor or neural trigger** that causes the human brain to generate a sensory anomaly (like a feeling of presence, auditory hallucination, or dizziness) which the person then attributes to a supernatural cause ("a ghost"). This is known as **misattribution**.
